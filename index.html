Introduction

The Yu-Gi-Oh! Trading Card game is a game based on the fictional game of Duel Monsters that takes place in the anime and manga franchise "Yu-Gi-Oh!" created by Kazuki Takahashi. Konami first launched the game in 1999 in Japan and later on launched the game in North America in 2002. As of January 2021, it is estimated that the franchsie has sold over 35 billion cards and have grossed over $9.64 billion.

Gameplay

A normal "duel" consists of two oposing players. Each player has a main deck containing at least 40 cards and no more than 60 cards. A deck can contain up to three copies of the same card. Additionally, for tournament play some cards on the forbidden, limited, or semi-limited list can either not be used in the deck, only have one copy in the deck, or only have two copies in the deck respectively. At the beginning of the duel, both players start with 8000 life points. There are four ways to win a duel:

  • Reduced the opposing player's life points to 0
  • A player is unable to draw a card from their deck when they're suppose to
  • A player meets the win conditions of a specific card effect to win a duel/match
  • A player surrenders
Game Mat

The Game Mat is where both players will play there cards. Its organized into different zones for where cards should be played. A physical game mat is not needed to play the game, but both players will need to remember where to place each specific card when played.

The zones on the game mat are classified in the following way:

Main Monster zone:

this is where monsters are played. Each player can have up to 5 monsters in the main monster zone and can be positioneed in 3 different way:

  • Face up attack position
  • Face up defense position
  • Face down defense position

A monster card is in attack position when the card is upright (portrait orientation) and in defense position when the card is sideways (landscape orientation)

Spell & Trap Zone and Pendulum Zone:

Like the monster zone, you can have have up to five Spell or Trap cards in this zone. This zone is located behind the Main Monster Zone. Spell and Trap cards must be activated face up or set face down from this zone. It is ok to place a monster card in this zone, if an effect says to do this.

Additionally, the far left and right spaces are known as the Pendulum zone. Spell and Trap cards can still be activated or set in these two zones. If a player intends to activate a Pendulum monster as Spell card, then they must place them in the Pendulum zone.

Field Zone

There are a special Spell Card noted by a specific symbal known as Field Spell cards. These cards can only be played in the Field Spell zone which is located to the far left of the Monster zone. Each player is only allowed one field spell at a time. A field spell can be activated face up or set face down in the field spell zone. When a player is ready to activate or set a new field spell, send the current field spell card to the Graveyard before placing or setting the new one.

Extra Deck

Cards in this deck are face down and is located behind the Field Card Zone. You may look at the cards in your own Extra Deck during the game. Pendulum monsters that would be sent from the Monster zone or Pendulum zone to the Graveyard would be sent face up to the Extra Deck instead. Monsters in this deck may be summoned once certain conditions are met.

Extra Monster Zone

These two zones are shared by both players. These two zones are located in front of the number two and number four Monster zones. Normally, each player may only use one of these zones at a time. Monsters from the Extra deck can be played in one of these zones. Link monsters that are Linked summoned or Pendulum monsters that are Pendulumed Summoned from the Extra Deck are played in the Extra Monster zone. Monsters played in the Extra Monster zone does not count towards a player's 5 monster limit.

Graveyard

After monster cards are destroyed or when Spell & Trap cards are used up, they are placed face up in the Graveyard in the order they're sent there (this order should not be changed). Either player can look through their Graveyard or their opponent's Graveyard

Banished Cards

Sometimes a card effect will banish card(s). If this happens then, the card will not go to the Graveyard. Normally players will start a pile of cards that are banished to the right of the Graveyard. Either player may look through their banished pile and must keep them in order of when they were banished (most recent banished card on top)

Deck

This is where your main deck will go face down. This is where you will draw your cards from. If an effect requires you to reveal cards from your deck or for you to look through your deck, always shuffle your deck one you are done before returning your deck face down. All cards in your deck should be face down unless a card effect that was played or is currently active says otherwise.

Monster Cards

Monster Cards are used to battle and defeat your opponent. Battles between Monster Cards are the foundation of any Duel. Monster cards are drawn from your main deck. Regular monster cards come in two types: normal and effect.

Normal monsters

These cards have a yellowish background. These are basic Monster Cards without special abilities. These cards may have a higher attack points and/or defesne points, but do not have any special abilites

Effect monsters

These cards have an orange background. These cards have a special ability. These effects can only be used when cards are face up on the field.

The type of effect is divided into five categories:

  • This effect activates immediately when the monster is summoned to the field and ends when the monster leaves the field. Monsters with 2000 or less ATK cannot declare an attack.
  • Ignition (you declare the activation of the effect)
    You can Tribute this card, then target 1 monster Example on the field; destroy that target.
  • Quick Effect - these effects can be activated during either player's turn and have a higher priority than other other effects.
    When an effect is activated (Quick Effect): You can target 1 card in your Graveyard; Special Summon it.
  • Trigger Effect - effects that trigger when conditions are met.
    When this card is destroyed by battle and sent to the Graveyard: Target 1 card on the field; destroy that target.
  • Flip Effect - when a face down card is flipped face up.
    FLIP: Draw 1 card.

A monster card is composed of several elements:

  • Card name - This is the card's name. If a card name is mentioned in card text, it will appear in quotations. Cards that have the same name are considered to be the same card.
  • Attribute - each monster card has an attribute and is sometimes important for card effects.
  • Level - the number of red/yellow stars equals the card's level. Monster level four and below can be placed in the monster zone without a tribute. Monsters level five and above require tributes to placed in the monster zone.
  • Artwork - all Yu-Gi-Oh! cards have an image representing the card. Some monster cards have alternate art work displayed, but are still counted towards the three card limit.
  • Type / Effect - All monster cards have a specific type. This is important for card effects that mention monster types. If a card has a special ability, the word "effect" appear to the right of the Monster type.
  • Description/Effect - For normal monsters text describing the monster is written here. For effect monsters, the special ability is written here.
  • ATK & DEF - ATK refers to the monster's starting attack points and DEF refers to the monster's starting defense points.
Spell and Trap Cards

Spell cards are effects that you will normally activate during your turn. Trap cards normally will need to be set during your turn and can be activated during your opponent's turn or your following turn. The effects of these cards vary from destroying other cards to strengthening monsters. Both Spell cards and Trap cards are drawn from your main deck. Spell cards have a green background while Trap cards have a purple background.

A Spell and Trap cards are composed of several elements:

  • Card name - This is the name of the Spell card. Cards of the same name count towards your three copy limit.
  • Type - Instead of an attribute like Monster cards, they have a type that says "SPELL 魔” symbol for Spell cards and Trap Cards has the “TRAP 罠” symbol.
  • Icon - there are 6 icons that represent special type of spell cards. Cards without these icons are normal spell cards.
  • Card description - Each card’s activation conditions and effects are provided here. Read the card description carefully and follow the instructions.
Link Monster Cards

A Link Monster has the ability to increase the number of monsters you can Summon from your Extra Deck. In addition to the 1 Extra Monster Zone you can usually use, you can also Summon monsters from the Extra Deck to any of your Main Monster Zones that has a Link Monster’s red arrow pointing to it.

Pendulum Monster Cards

Pendulum monsters are both Monsters and Spell cards. They can be Summoned as monsters to attack or defend, or you can activate them as Spell Cards in your Pendulum Zones to activate extra special abilities and allow you to Pendulum Summon. Pendulum monsters are placed in your Main deck. If a Pendulum monster card is on the field and is destroyed, the card is placed face up on the Extra deck instead.

Pendulum cards background are two toned. Normal Pendulum Monsters have a yellow top have half that fades to green. Effect Pendulum Monsters have an orange top half that fades to green.

Pendulm cards have some added elements to their cards:

  • Pendulum effect - this is the first box below the card image. This is the effect that is applied if the the Pendulum monster is placed in the Pendulum zone and used as a Spell card. Not all Pendulum monsters will have a Pendulum effect.
  • Pendulum scale - This is a number that determines which monsters you can Pendulum summon during your turn.

Pendulum Summon

If you have two cards in your Pendulum zones, you can Pendulum summon during your turn. To Pendulum summon, check the scales of both Pendulum cards. For example if the Pendulum scale on the left side is a 2 and the Pendulum scale on the right is 8, then you can Pendulum Summon monsters level 3 through 7 from your hand. You can also Pendulum Summon any face up Pendulum monsters that are within the Pendulum scale to an extra monster zone.

XYZ Monster Cards

XYZ Monsters (pronounced “ik-seez”) are monsters you can Summon whenever you control monsters that are the same Level that meets an XYZ Monsters requirements. XYZ Monsters start in your Extra Deck, not in your Main Deck. XYZ monsters have a black background and do not have a level; instead they have a rank with blakc and yellow stars on the left side. The cards used for an XYZ summon must be face up on the field and become XYZ materials. Stack the XYZ materials on top of each other and place the XYZ monster on top of the materials. Some effects of XYZ monsters requires XYZ material to be detached from the monster.

Synchro Monster Cards

Synchro Monsters are powerful cards that can be summoned to the field by using the Levels of your monsters. They can be Synchro Summoned from the Extra Deck by sending 1 face-up “Tuner” monster and any number of face-up non-Tuner monsters from your field to the Graveyard, when the sum of all their Levels is exactly equal to the Level of the Synchro Monster or meet the reuirements for the synchro summon. Synchro monsters are placed in your extra deck and have a while background.

Ritual Monster Cards

Ritual Monsters are special monsters that are Special Summoned with a specific Ritual Spell Card, along with a required Tribute. Ritual Monster Cards are drawn from the Main Deck and cannot be Summoned unless you have all the proper cards together in your hand or on the field. Ritual Monsters generally have high ATK and DEF and some have special abilities. Ritual monster cards have a blue background and to the right of the type it says "Ritual".

Fusion Monster Cards

Fusion Monsters are summoned by using the specific monsters listed on the card (called Fusion Materials) combined with a Summoning card like "Polymerization". They usually have special abilities and very high Attack Points as well. Fusion monsters are placed in your extra deck and have a purple background. Since Fusion Summons require specific cards, be sure to include those cards in your Main Deck.
